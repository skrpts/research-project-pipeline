## Overview

Academic integrity is the commitment to honest, responsible scholarship. This policy outlines the principles and practices that ensure research and writing meet ethical standards.

## Core Principles

### Honesty
- Present your own work as your own
- Acknowledge all sources of ideas, data, and quotations
- Report research findings accurately

### Attribution
- Cite all sources used in your work
- Use quotation marks for direct quotes
- Paraphrase in your own words and still cite the source

### Originality
- Submit original work for each assignment
- Do not recycle work from previous submissions without permission
- Contribute meaningfully to group projects

## What Constitutes Plagiarism

- Copying text without quotation marks and citation
- Paraphrasing too closely to the original without citation
- Submitting another person's work as your own
- Using AI-generated content without disclosure

## Best Practices

- Keep detailed records of all sources consulted
- Use a reference manager to track citations
- When in doubt, cite the source
- Allow time for proper referencing before submission
