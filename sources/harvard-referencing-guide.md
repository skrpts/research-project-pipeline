## Overview

The Harvard referencing system is an author-date citation style widely used in academic writing. This guide covers the essential formatting rules.

## In-Text Citations

- Single author: (Smith, 2023)
- Two authors: (Smith and Jones, 2023)
- Three or more: (Smith et al., 2023)
- Direct quote: (Smith, 2023, p. 42)
- Multiple works: (Smith, 2023; Jones, 2022)

## Reference List Format

### Books
Author, A.A. (Year) *Title of Book*. Edition. Place: Publisher.

### Journal Articles
Author, A.A. (Year) 'Title of article', *Journal Name*, Volume(Issue), pp. pages.

### Websites
Author/Organisation (Year) *Title of page*. Available at: URL (Accessed: date).

## Key Rules

- Reference list is alphabetical by author surname
- Use hanging indent for entries longer than one line
- Italicise titles of books, journals, and websites
- Use single quotation marks for article and chapter titles
